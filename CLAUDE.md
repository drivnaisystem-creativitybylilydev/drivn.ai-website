## THE BUSINESS MODEL (TL;DR)

**What We Do:** Build complete AI automation systems for home service businesses (website + AI receptionist + lead qualification + chatbot + reviews + quoting + dashboard)

**Pricing Tiers:**
- Tier 1: Website + Review Agent = $800 setup + $100/mo
- Tier 2: Growth System (most popular) = $3,500-$5,000 setup + $500-$750/mo
- Tier 3: Full Custom = $7,500-$10,000+ setup + $1,000-$2,000/mo
- Tier 4: Revenue Share = 10-20% of revenue generated (high-volume only)

**6-Month Target:**
- $2,500/month MRR (minimum)
- $15,000 in setup fees (minimum)
- 3-5 clients with proven case studies
- Repeatable delivery system documented

**Implementation Timeline:**
- Tier 1 (website only): 10-14 days
- Tier 2-3 (full system): ~30 days
- No developers yet (Finn does delivery)

## STRATEGIC GUARDRAILS

### What This Means for Decision-Making

**ALLOWED:**
- Targeting home service contractors (multiple niches within this)
- Qualifying prospects using the discovery questions
- Closing clients on Tier 2 (the sweet spot)
- Collecting case studies and ROI data
- Optimizing systems based on client feedback
- Planning for developer hire (Month 4+)

**NOT ALLOWED:**
- Underpricing (Tier 2 minimum $3,500 setup, not $1,200)
- Custom scope creep beyond the tier definitions
- Taking clients with <10 calls/week (wrong fit)
- Multi-niche testing (validate 1-2 niches first, then scale)
- Revenue share unless they do $50K+/month in potential business
- Launching without 30 days of support (systems will have bugs)

---

### When You See These Red Flags

**Finn says:** "Should we also test real estate agents?"
**Response:** "Not yet. Pick 1-2 home service niches and validate them first (close 3-5 clients each). Once you have case studies and a repeatable system, expand to new niches. Spreading thin doesn't work."

**Finn says:** "We can offer this for $1,500 setup to be competitive."
**Response:** "That underprices the value. You're delivering $10K-100K in annual revenue to the client. Tier 2 is $3,500 minimum. If they can't afford it, they're not the right fit yet—find bigger pain point clients."

**Finn says:** "This client needs custom workflows we don't have."
**Response:** "That's scope creep. Stay within Tier 2 deliverables. If they need more, that's Tier 3 (higher price). Custom builds = custom prices."

**Finn says:** "Someone with 5 calls/week wants us to build them a system."
**Response:** "Not a fit. They need lead generation first, not efficiency automation. They'll be frustrated when the system captures calls they don't have. Focus on 15+ calls/week targets."

## SALES POSITIONING (Memorize)

**The Opening:**
"Most contractors miss 30-50% of their inbound calls. That's leaving serious money on the table. The ones who don't miss—they close more deals and grow faster. That's what we do: capture those leads, qualify them, and get them into your sales pipeline so you just show up and close."

**The Qualifier:**
"How many calls do you get per week?" 
- <10: Not a fit (wrong problem)
- 10-20: Tier 1-2
- 20-50: Tier 2-3
- 50+: Tier 3 or Revenue Share

**The Promise:**
"30 days to launch. After 30 days, you'll see more calls captured, more qualified leads, more revenue. If the numbers don't show, we adjust it for free. This is a partnership based on results, not a product you hope works."
