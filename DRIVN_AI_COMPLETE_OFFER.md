## SALES POSITIONING & VALUE PROP

### The Core Message

**NOT:** "We answer your phones"
**YES:** "We help you generate more revenue through AI automation"

### The Sales Script (Customize with Their Data)

**Discovery Call Opening:**
"Most contractors are missing 30-50% of their inbound calls. That's leaving money on the table. The ones who don't miss calls—they close more deals and grow faster. That's what we do: capture those leads, qualify them, and get them into your sales pipeline so all you do is show up and close them."

**The Value Calculation:**
"You probably get about [X] calls per week. Of those, you miss about [Y]—either because you're in the field or it goes to voicemail. At your average job value of $[Z], that's about $[revenue loss] per month you're leaving on the table. Our system captures those leads for a fraction of that cost."

**The ROI Promise:**
"We'll implement in 30 days. After 30 days, you'll see:
- [X]% of missed calls now captured and followed up
- [Y]% increase in qualified appointments
- $[Z] in additional revenue from leads we captured
- [Time] hours saved per week on admin and follow-ups
If the numbers don't add up, we adjust the system until they do."

### Qualifying Questions (Use on Discovery Call)

Ask these to determine which tier they fit:

1. **"How many calls/inquiries do you get per week?"**
   - <10: Not a fit yet (lead gen problem, not efficiency)
   - 10-20: Tier 1 or 2
   - 20-50: Tier 2 or 3
   - 50+: Tier 3 or Revenue Share

2. **"How many are you currently missing or not following up on?"**
   - This determines impact size and price

3. **"What's your average job value?"**
   - <$500: Not a fit (margin too thin)
   - $500-$1.5K: Tier 1-2
   - $1.5K-$5K: Tier 2-3
   - $5K+: Tier 3 or Revenue Share

4. **"How much time do you spend per week on admin, quoting, following up?"**
   - This helps sell the "time saved" value

5. **"Are you open to AI automation, or is this your first time?"**
   - Determines messaging and education needed

### Disqualifiers (Don't Waste Time)

❌ **<10 calls/week** — They need lead generation, not automation
❌ **Bad Google reviews (<3.5 stars)** — We can't fix service quality, only capture
❌ **Not open to tech** — Will be high-maintenance, low-success
❌ **Already have receptionist** — No pain point (unless receptionist is expensive)
❌ **"What's your cheapest option?"** — Price-focused, not results-focused
❌ **National chain/corporate** — Different buying process, different needs

### Current Status
- 127 leads scraped across multiple niches
- Some don't have websites (good entry point)
- Different niches have different pain points

### How to Choose (Use Jarvis)

**Consult Jarvis on:**
1. "Which of these 127 leads has the biggest pain point we can solve?"
2. "Which niche has the tightest network (highest referral potential)?"
3. "Which niche is most receptive to tech adoption?"
4. "Which 1-2 niches should we focus on first?"

### Scoring Criteria (After Jarvis Input)

- **Pain Point Size** — How much revenue are they losing? (Higher = better)
- **Network Effect** — Do they talk to each other? (Referrals compound)
- **Tech Receptiveness** — Are they open to AI? (Lower friction = faster sales)
- **Scalability** — Can we find 100+ in this niche? (Supports growth)
- **Price Acceptance** — Can they afford $3K-5K setup? (Market fit)

### Testing Process

Once Jarvis identifies 1-2 niches:
1. Target 5-10 leads from that niche
2. Close 2-3 clients
3. Collect case studies and ROI data
4. Use proof to scale to 10-20 clients in that niche
5. Once validated, expand to next niche or scale horizontally
